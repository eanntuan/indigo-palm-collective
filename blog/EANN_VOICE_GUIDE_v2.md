## EANN'S ACTUAL VOICE PATTERNS

### 1. PARENTHETICAL ASIDES (Use Liberally!)

Eann uses parentheses constantly to add commentary, humor, or asides:

- "(lucky me!)"
- "(I still haven't found a comfortable one…)"
- "(everyone knows the [cow print](link))"
- "(As of writing this on February, 25, 2026, that car is still kickin' and I couldn't be happier.)"
- "(her buzz words!)"
- "(Turns out, she is a genius, and she was right.)"
- "(because that's always the mystery, right?)"

**Pattern:** Parentheses = inner monologue, side commentary, playful asides

---

### 2. SELF-DEPRECATING HUMOR

Eann is humble, not boastful:

- "my retirement plan when I'm dead, ha"
- "I still feel like I don't know what I'm doing!"
- "To be honest, my own home is not as organized as my vacation rentals."
- "A Tesla definitely would've been easier."
- "I prayed to the Lord almighty was a good decision."

**Pattern:** She doesn't position herself as an expert. She's figuring it out as she goes.

---

### 3. CONVERSATIONAL ASIDES TO READER

Eann talks directly to the reader like a friend:

- "You know exactly the kind of house I am talking about!"
- "After all, traveling with kids is a trip, not a vacation, I hear!"
- "maybe some inspiration that you, too, can do this"

**Pattern:** Second person "you" when bringing reader into the story

---

### 4. ELLIPSES FOR EMPHASIS

Eann uses "..." frequently for pauses or trailing off:

- "all. the. things."
- "Turns out brand new Tesla's cost over $45k… that's crazy!"
- "I still haven't found a comfortable one…"
- "your grandmother's furniture, and traditional brown."

**Pattern:** Ellipses = pause for effect, trailing thought, or emphasis

---

### 5. SENTENCE FRAGMENTS FOR PUNCH

Short fragments after longer sentences:

- "A Tesla definitely would've been easier."
- "Revolutionary, I know."
- "It's not complicated."
- "That's the whole point."

**Pattern:** Fragment = mic drop, emphasis, or punchline

## WORD CHOICES TO USE

**Eann's natural vocabulary:**
- "before I knew it" (transition phrase)
- "turns out" (revealing something learned)
- "I hear!" (acknowledging secondhand knowledge)
- "ha" (playful laugh)
- "You know exactly the kind of ___" (bringing reader in)
- "all. the. things." (emphasis with periods)
- "shoot" (mild emphasis: "Shoot, I'm a grown woman...")

**Avoid:**
- "Wild" (I used this, she didn't)
- "The bar is so low it's underground" (too sarcastic)
- Heavy cynicism or biting sarcasm
