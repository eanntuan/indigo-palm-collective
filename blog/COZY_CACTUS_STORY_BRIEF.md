## Voice & Tone Guidelines

**FIRST PERSON - Write as EANN:**
- Use I/me/my throughout: "I built The Cozy Cactus because...", "Here's what I noticed...", "When I started hosting..."
- Direct, no-BS, grounded
- Calls out problems: "Most vacation rentals are either corporate sterile or drowning in throw pillows with inspirational quotes. I didn't want that."
- Practical with edge: "Here's what actually works..."
- Honest takes: "I'm not trying to create a sanctuary. But families keep calling it that."
- Personal anecdotes: "When a mom messaged me at 11pm asking about bottle warmers, I realized..."

**NOT Dawn's Voice (Avoid):**
- Third person: "Eann Tuan delivers...", "The founder created..."
- Poetic/feel-y language like "soothing balm," "sensory sanctuary"
- "Spaces that feel alive yet calming, expressive yet effortless"
- Flowery descriptions

## Suggested Structure

### Intro (150-200 words)
Hook with the problem IN FIRST PERSON: "I watched families check into vacation rentals and just relocate their chaos to a new zip code. They weren't resting. They were surviving. So I built The Cozy Cactus to solve that specific problem."

### Section 1: "The Problem I Kept Seeing"
Write in first person: "I started hosting because I kept watching families check into vacation rentals and just... relocate their chaos. Nobody was thinking about families with infants. Hotels had cribs but no thought. Airbnbs had 'kid-friendly' listings but it was just a pack-n-play in the corner. That wasn't rest. That was survival in a different zip code."

### Section 2: "What I Built Instead"
Walk through YOUR specific decisions in first person:
- "I created a labeling system. Everything labeled: where the batteries are, where the extra linens go, which drawer has the baby spoons."
- "I bought infant gear that actually works - not cheap Amazon basics that break by checkout."
- "I set up the kitchen for families, not just wine glasses and a corkscrew."
- "I designed the pool area for actual supervision, not Instagram."

### Section 3: "The Details That Matter"
The stuff guests notice after they arrive (first person):
- "I organize the drawers. Everything has a place."
- "I respond in under 1 minute. Always have."
- "I put out things you don't have to ask for because they're already there."

### Section 4: "What Guests Actually Say"
Pull from real reviews but introduce in first person:
- "A family of 5 told me it was the first time they 'truly rested' on vacation."
- "A 10-year Airbnb veteran said my labeling system was unprecedented."
- "I have repeat guests. Even grown dudes who come back."

### Conclusion (100-150 words)
Circle back in first person: "I wasn't trying to be revolutionary. I was just solving a problem I kept seeing - families need to rest, not perform vacation. That's it. That's The Cozy Cactus."

**Soft CTA:** "Ready to actually rest with your family? Book The Cozy Cactus."
