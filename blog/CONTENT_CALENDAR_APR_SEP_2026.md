## Content Notes

### From the Dawn Call (March 13, 2026)

**The newsletter-first strategy is load-bearing.**
Dawn was explicit: no bookings came from Instagram. Her direct advice was "Pinterest and newsletter" — newsletter because Eann can write it over days and weeks, newsletter because it drives direct bookings, newsletter because it suits Eann's personality and doesn't drain her. Every post in this calendar should have a newsletter CTA. At least 4 posts per cycle should be repurposed as newsletter issues (not just linked — rewritten into personal newsletter voice).

**Pinterest is the distribution channel, not Instagram.**
Dawn's VA tripled her Pinterest viewership. Specific itinerary posts (48 Hours in Indio, Stagecoach planning, pet-friendly guide) are exactly the formats that Pinterest algorithms favor. When writing blog posts, think "does this make a good static pin?" and write the headline accordingly. The August and September posts are especially Pinterest-optimized. See the Pinterest Pin Ideas column in the calendar table for specific pin title suggestions for each post.

**Eann's personal identity belongs in the content.**
Dawn pushed hard on this: Eann's surfing life, her Culver City base, her wanderlust personality are underused in marketing. The Sundune brand guide includes it prominently. The blog has not leaned into it yet. Starting in June, posts should begin to weave in Eann's personal frame more directly — not in a forced way, but in the same natural-digression style the voice guides describe. The "three years in" post in September is the natural home for this. The Surf Club post (Post 10) is a natural extension of the surfing identity.

**Dawn's 6 brand filters are the best editorial lens available.**
For Casa Moto posts specifically, any "Design & Culture" piece should be written with the 6 filters visible in the structure — not necessarily listed, but used as the organizing logic. Cocooned & Soothing, Vibrant & Rejuvenating, Cultured, Rhythmic, Effortless Ease, Grounded. When a post doesn't have an obvious angle, ask: which filter is this post expressing?

**The photo shoot on May 14 is the content inflection point.**
Posts 1–3 are written without Casa Moto's final photos. Posts 4 onward have them. Don't try to visually anchor Casa Moto content before May 23 — write around the renovation process instead (which is itself the story). The May 9 staging post is specifically designed to use behind-the-scenes/process images rather than requiring final hero shots.

**The Indigo Palm Collective map/brochure is a future content asset.**
Dawn and Eann discussed building a printed illustrated map covering the full Indio-to-Palm-Springs corridor for all properties. When it exists, it becomes a blog post ("We Made a Map of the Coachella Valley"), a newsletter issue, and a Pinterest pin. Flag for Q4 2026 or Q1 2027 depending on timeline. The July 25 "48 Hours in Indio" post and the Field Guide Source List below plant the content seeds for that brochure.

### Voice Reminders (from EANN_VOICE_GUIDE_v2.md)

These are the most commonly violated patterns based on existing draft reviews — worth checking for every post before publishing:

- **Open with personal backstory, not the problem.** Eann's posts wander into the topic. They don't start with it.
- **Parenthetical asides are not optional.** "(because that's always the mystery, right?)" — they appear constantly and are part of what makes the voice feel like Eann.
- **Credit Dawn by name and link every time.** Not "my designer" — "Dawn Asher, of The Olive Jar."
- **No emojis in blog post body.** Email and texts, yes. Blog posts, no.
- **"Before I knew it" and "turns out" are natural transitions.** Use them.
- **Fragments are punctuation.** "Revolutionary, I know." is not a typo.
- **Specific brands get links.** Stokke. The Olive Jar. L&G Desert Store. Link them.
- **Self-deprecation over authority.** "I still feel like I don't know what I'm doing!" is the brand voice. It is not weakness. Do not iron it out.
