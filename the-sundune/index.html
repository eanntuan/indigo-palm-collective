The Sundune

Palm Springs, California

Fresh, nostalgic, effortlessly Palm Springs.

Coastal Desert Ease. Three KING Beds.

The Sundune at Palm Springs is a coastal-inspired desert hideaway with 3 KING beds where ease meets exploration — fresh, nostalgic, and effortlessly Palm Springs — just minutes from both The Surf Club and the heart of downtown. The kind of place most people stop looking once they find it.

The design runs on a coastal-desert frequency: a design-forward beach bungalow — without cliché coastal décor. Sun-bleached textures, natural materials, a palette that pulls from surf and sand. Real retro personality. Copper and cool aloe. Early-morning light through linen curtains. The feeling of a weekend that actually slows down. Not trendy. Not trying too hard. Just good design that makes you want to stay in.

Three full king beds means three couples can share without anyone drawing straws over who gets the pull-out. Full kitchen, and a living room that doesn't make you feel like you're camping. Walk out the door and you're in it — Palm Canyon Drive, coffee, galleries, restaurants, all on foot. This is the version of Palm Springs that regulars come back to.

The Sundune is nostalgic in the right way — it doesn't recreate a specific decade, it captures a feeling. A basecamp for those ready to hang up their surfboard, sleep in a little longer, and head out inspired. Long summers. Road trips. Salt air. The kind of vacation where you lose track of the date and consider staying an extra night because leaving feels wrong. Wanderlust, active — and also very cocktail chill.

Location & Getting Around

The Sundune is in central Palm Springs — walking distance to downtown. Palm Canyon Drive, the Uptown Design District, restaurants, coffee, galleries, vintage shops: all on foot. This is the part of Palm Springs people mean when they say they love Palm Springs. You're not in the outskirts wondering if you need an Uber for everything. You're in it.

The Surf Club Palm Springs is minutes away — one of the more fun day-trip amenities in the valley if you've never been. Downtown dining is walkable: casual spots, proper restaurants, the bar scene. Coachella and Stagecoach are about 30 minutes east (Empire Polo Club). Joshua Tree National Park is 45 minutes away. Indian Wells Tennis Garden for the BNP Paribas Open is 25 minutes. Modernism Week events center in Palm Springs — this property puts you in the middle of all of it.

Who Stays Here

Three couples splitting a trip. A group of friends who want separate beds without sacrificing a good location. People who've done the big hotel and want something with more personality. Couples who come to Palm Springs for the design, the food, the hiking, and the vibe — and want a home base that fits all of it. Anyone who's searched "Palm Springs vacation rental walkable to downtown" and been disappointed by the results. The Sundune is what you were actually looking for.
