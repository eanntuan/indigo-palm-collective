Getting There

Walking to the Festival

You made it. Months of planning and the week is finally here. Good news: you have the best location. You're inside Indian Palms Country Club, which puts you within walking distance of the festival. No long shuttle lines, no crazy surge pricing.

The Yellow Path

The Yellow Path at 49th and Monroe is your best bet for walking to the festival. End to end it's about a mile and a half, so bring comfortable shoes since the path is gravel.

The Monroe gate is at 48th and Monroe. From Terra Luz at 49768 Pacino, it's about a 1.2 mile / 27 minute walk to the front gate, then an additional 0.3 miles to the Yellow Path. Signs throughout the community show the route.

Get Walking Directions

Biking May Be the Easiest Option

Seriously. Grab a cheap bike on Craigslist before the festival, bring a lock, and bike directly to the designated bike parking at the entrance. No gravel path, no waiting for a golf cart, no surge pricing.

You're 1.2 miles from the front gate. On a bike that's under 10 minutes. Grab a $50 beater, lock it up, and sell or donate it on your way out.

Note on Pedestrian Restrictions

No pedestrians allowed on Monroe (between Ave 49 and Ave 52) or Madison (between Ave 50 and Ave 52).

Inside the Community

Indian Palms

You're inside Indian Palms Country Club, a gated community that borders the festival grounds on Monroe. The front gate (your exit to the Yellow Path) is on Monroe at 48th.

Gate Rules

The only gate you are permitted to walk through is on Monroe (the main gate). Signs throughout the community show the path to get there.

The festival drop-off area for Ubers and street vehicles is on Monroe, about half a mile from the main gate. From that drop-off point it's roughly a 1.5 mile walk to the main festival entrance.

Food and Drinks at the Gate

There are food and drink vendors in the parking lot near the front gate, along with a restaurant and bar called Hangar 19. The BBQ spot stays open late, even after the festival wraps up. Good to know for the walk back.

Community Amenities While You're Here

When you're not at the festival, Indian Palms has more to offer:

  • 3 community pools
  • Pickleball courts
  • Tennis courts (ID required to rent rackets)
  • Lifestyle Center / fitness center (reach out and we'll get you set up)

Saving Your Feet

Golf Cart Rides

Many local residents offer rides via cars, trucks, or golf carts for a fee. Important distinction: golf carts can only take you to the front gate of Indian Palms. Vehicles (trucks, SUVs) can take you all the way to the festival drop-off site on Monroe. Ask when you call where exactly they drop off.

At the end of the night, golf carts will be waiting at the front gate offering rides home for $10 per person. Worth every dollar if your feet are done.

Plan ahead. These contacts are word of mouth, not officially endorsed. Expect to pay around $5-$10 per person each way, but confirm the rate with the driver when you call.

If You Must Drive

Driving

It gets extremely congested around festival hours. Avoid driving if at all possible. If you must, plan for long waits and avoid 48th and Monroe during peak egress.

GateDetails
Monroe Gate (Indian Palms) Main gate for guest entry. Security staff in attendance. Extra workstation added to double processing capacity. Transponder-only lane converts to allow general guest access during peak hours.
Ave 48 + Jackson Gates Operate as usual with no security officers. Entry via Conekt app or license plate. Recommended to minimize traffic delays.
Avenue 50 Gate Restricted by Indio Police to residents only. Do not use this gate.

Road Closures

Closed Streets

Starting April 6th, Ave 50 between Madison and Monroe will experience intermittent closures and delays. Full closures occur Friday through Sunday on April 13-16, 20-23, and 27-30. Streets reopen to normal traffic May 4th.

  • Avenue 49 between Monroe and Hjorth
  • Madison between Ave. 49 and 50
  • Madison between Ave. 50 and 52 during egress nightly (9:30pm to 3:00am)
