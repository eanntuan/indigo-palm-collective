# Indigo Palm Collective - Blog Content Strategy

**Mission:** Drive organic traffic, build brand authority, convert searchers into bookers.

---

## Voice & Style Guide

**Tone:**
- Direct, no-BS, grounded in reality
- Authentic, anti-pretentious
- Slightly cynical but helpful ("tired of generic," "need actual rest")
- Practical with personality
- Honest positioning over flowery language

**Avoid:**
- Poetic/feel-y language ("soothing balm," "sensory sanctuary")
- Generic travel blog fluff
- "Top 10" clickbait (unless genuinely valuable)
- Instagram influencer voice
- Overly promotional or flowery language

**What Works:**
- Calling out problems directly ("families who need actual rest," "design lovers tired of generic")
- Practical value with edge ("Here's what actually works...")
- Honest takes ("Most travel blogs won't tell you this...")
- Specific, useful details (exact restaurants, real prices, actual distances)
- Conversational intelligence (smart but accessible)

## Blog Structure (Template)

### Standard Post Format:

**Intro (150-200 words):**
- Hook with sensory detail or question
- Set emotional tone
- Promise value (what reader will learn/feel)

**Body (800-1,200 words):**
- 3-5 main sections with H2 headers
- Personal anecdotes or specific examples
- Actionable takeaways
- Cultural or design depth (not surface-level)

**Conclusion (100-150 words):**
- Circle back to opening hook
- Emotional closure
- Soft CTA (not pushy)

**CTAs (End of Post):**
- "Ready to experience this yourself? Explore Casa Moto →"
- "Book your desert sanctuary at indigopalm.co"
- Link to relevant property page

## Writing Guidelines (For Blog-Writer Agent)

### Voice Examples (FIRST PERSON - Write as Eann):

**Good (Direct, Practical, Slightly Cynical, First Person):**
> "Most Coachella house listings are either $5,000/night or look like your uncle's RV. I didn't want either. So I built something else: three bedrooms, actual design, 15 minutes to the festival grounds, and you don't have to share a porta-potty with 80,000 strangers. Here's what you need to know."

**Bad (too feel-y, avoid):**
> "The sun hits differently here. Not sharp, not scorching—more like a warm hand on your shoulder, reminding you to slow down."

---

**Good (Honest, Useful, First Person):**
> "L&G Desert Store sells dates. Really good ones. Medjool dates that cost $12/pound and are worth every cent. It's 8 minutes from my property. They also have date shakes. Get one. That's the entire recommendation."

**Bad (generic travel blog, avoid):**
> "If you're looking for the best dates in Indio, check out L&G Desert Store! It's a must-visit spot with tons of great options that you'll absolutely love!"

---

**Good (Calls out the problem, First Person):**
> "Here's the thing about most vacation rentals: they're either corporate sterile or trying way too hard to be 'boho.' I designed my properties for people tired of both. Real furniture, actual design decisions, no macrame plant hangers. For adults who want to rest, not perform rest for Instagram."

**Bad (promotional fluff, avoid):**
> "Casa Moto is an amazing vacation rental with a beautiful pool and great amenities! You'll love the stunning design and incredible atmosphere!"

---

### Sentence Structure:
- **Vary length:** Mix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones
- **Use em dashes:** For emphasis—like this
- **Start with verbs:** "Picture this..." "Imagine..." "Consider..."
- **Specific over generic:** "pass-through kitchen window" not "open kitchen"

### Cultural References:
- ✅ Old Havana patina, wabi-sabi warmth, conga rhythms
- ✅ Dawn Asher (The Olive Jar), Kahlo Blue
- ❌ Generic "boho," "eclectic," "rustic charm"

### Audience Address:
- **Write as "I" (Eann)** talking to "you" (the reader)
- **First person throughout:** "I built this because...", "When I started hosting...", "Here's what I learned..."
- **Assume intelligence:** Don't over-explain
- **Honor values:** Intention, presence, authenticity, design integrity
- **Reject:** Generic travel, Instagram tourism, performative relaxation
