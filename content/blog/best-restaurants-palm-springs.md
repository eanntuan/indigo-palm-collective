<p>I've spent enough time in Palm Springs to have opinions about where to eat. These are places I've actually been to, not a list compiled from press releases. I organized them by meal because the city runs on a rhythm: what you want at 7am is different from what you want at 9pm.</p>

<p>A few geographic notes before we start. Downtown Palm Springs is the main dining cluster along Palm Canyon Drive and its side streets. The Uptown Design District, about a mile north on North Palm Canyon, has a more relaxed, local feel and some of the better wine and cocktail options. North Palm Springs is the stretch farther up the 111 corridor toward Cathedral City, home to a few anchor spots worth knowing. The restaurants below are scattered across all three zones.</p>

<h2>Coffee and Morning</h2>

<h3>Koffi</h3>

<p>Koffi has been in Palm Springs for over 20 years and that longevity isn't an accident. The outdoor patio at the North Palm Canyon location is the best morning seat in the city: shade trees, good light, the easy energy of a place where people come to linger rather than grab and go. The cold brew is the move in warm months. The iced latte works year-round. Multiple locations in the valley, but the North Palm Canyon one has the best setup.</p>

<p>Check current hours at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/koffi/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@koffi on Instagram</a>. They close earlier than you'd expect on some days.</p>

<h3>Cartel Coffee Lab</h3>

<p>Cartel is an Arizona-born specialty roaster with a spot inside the Arrive Hotel at 1551 N Palm Canyon Drive. Direct trade, specific origins, nothing blended to please everyone. The cold brew is excellent. The hotel design is worth a look on its own, and the coffee matches it. Find them at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/cartelcoffeecompany/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@cartelcoffeecompany</a>.</p>

<img src="/blog/images/cartel-coffee-palm-springs.jpg" alt="Cartel Coffee Lab at Arrive Hotel on North Palm Canyon Drive in Palm Springs" width="1400" height="933" loading="lazy">
<p class="image-caption">Cartel Coffee inside the Arrive Hotel. The room looks as good as the espresso.</p>

<h2>Brunch</h2>

<h3>Cheeky's</h3>

<p>Cheeky's is the Palm Springs brunch institution. The menu rotates weekly, built around seasonal ingredients, which keeps it fresh and also keeps regulars coming back to see what changed. The bacon flight is not a joke: four preparations of bacon, each different, and it's genuinely the right way to start an indulgent morning. The frittata is usually excellent. The room is small. Weekend waits are real.</p>

<p>Go on a weekday if you can. If it's a Saturday, arrive by 9am or expect to wait. Walk-ins only. Check <a href="https://www.instagram.com/cheekysps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@cheekysps</a> for current hours.</p>

<h3>Lulu California Bistro</h3>

<p>Lulu's works well when you have a group that can't agree on anything. It's a big, warm, loud room on Indian Canyon Drive with a menu that covers enough ground for everyone: eggs Benedict, omelets, cocktails, pastries. Good for families, good for groups who like a reliable yes when someone asks "where do you want to go." Check them at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/lulusps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@lulusps</a>.</p>

<h2>Lunch</h2>

<h3>Workshop Kitchen + Bar</h3>

<p>Workshop is a converted historic building with high ceilings, exposed concrete, and a food program that takes itself seriously. The cauliflower dish, when it's on, is worth ordering. The flatbread changes but is usually good. The cocktail program is one of the better ones in town. It works well for a relaxed lunch or an early dinner. Reservations help on weekends. Find them at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/workshopps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@workshopps</a>.</p>

<h3>Rooster and the Pig</h3>

<p>Vietnamese fusion in a small, loud room in the Uptown Design District. The pork belly bun is the thing to order. The lychee cocktails are better than they sound. Tables are close together and the service is fast. It fills up quickly; go early or go late. Check <a href="https://www.instagram.com/roosterandthepigps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@roosterandthepigps</a> for current hours.</p>

<h2>Dinner</h2>

<h3>Thai Hot</h3>

<p>Thai Hot is on Garnet Avenue and has no interest in being anything other than what it is: honest Thai food where the spice levels actually mean something. If you order medium spicy, it's medium spicy. The pad see ew is excellent. The larb is better than you'll find at most places in the valley. It is not fancy. That's the point. Check <a href="https://www.instagram.com/thaihot_ps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@thaihot_ps</a> for current hours.</p>

<img src="/blog/images/thai-hot.jpg" alt="Thai Hot restaurant exterior on Garnet Avenue in Palm Springs with warm interior lighting at night" width="1400" height="933" loading="lazy">
<p class="image-caption">Thai Hot. Order the pad see ew. Trust the spice level you ask for.</p>

<h3>Yes Please</h3>

<p>Yes Please is a natural wine bar with small plates and a genuinely good cheese situation. The vibe is low-key and the pour sizes are honest. Good for a slow evening when you want something thoughtful without committing to a full reservation. The crowd skews local. Find them at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/yespleaseps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@yespleaseps</a>.</p>

<img src="/blog/images/yes-please-palm-springs.jpg" alt="Yes Please wine bar interior in Palm Springs with warm lighting and natural wine bottles" width="1400" height="933" loading="lazy">
<p class="image-caption">Yes Please. Natural wines, small plates, no rush. This is what a Tuesday evening in Palm Springs should feel like.</p>

<h3>Trio Restaurant</h3>

<p>Trio is the Palm Springs restaurant that reliably works for every group configuration. American comfort food done well, a long menu, good cocktails. It's not trying to be the most exciting place in town. It's trying to be the most dependable one. For a dinner where you have five people with five different preferences, Trio is a safe call. Check <a href="https://www.instagram.com/triops/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@triops</a> for reservations.</p>

<h2>Late Night or Special Occasion</h2>

<h3>Rosemary Hi-Fi</h3>

<p>Rosemary Hi-Fi is a record store, restaurant, and bar hybrid in the Uptown Design District. The concept sounds like it shouldn't work, and then it does. The food is good: shared plates, a thoughtful menu, nothing overcomplicated. The vinyl is actually playing, not background noise. It's one of the more distinctly Palm Springs experiences on this list in the sense that it could only exist here. Find them at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/rosemaryhifi/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@rosemaryhifi</a>.</p>

<img src="/blog/images/rosemary-hifi.jpg" alt="Rosemary Hi-Fi interior in Palm Springs with record shelves, warm lighting, and dining tables" width="1400" height="933" loading="lazy">

<h2>Practical Notes</h2>

<p>Palm Springs restaurants get busy October through May. A few things worth knowing before you go:</p>

<p>Check Instagram for current hours before making a trip. Hours shift seasonally and some spots close for parts of the summer. Downtown parking is manageable if you use the structure on Amado Road. Park once and walk everything in the downtown and Uptown Design District stretch. For Thai Hot and spots on Garnet, street parking is usually fine.</p>

<p>Reservations matter at Workshop and Rosemary Hi-Fi on weekends. Cheeky's and Thai Hot are walk-in only. For everywhere else, calling ahead on a Friday or Saturday is worth the minute it takes.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>What are the best restaurants in Palm Springs for brunch?</h3>
<p>Cheeky's is the most well-known, with a rotating seasonal menu and a bacon flight that people plan trips around. Lulu's is the better choice for groups. Both are worth knowing. Cheeky's is walk-in only, so arrive early on weekends.</p>

<h3>Are there good dinner options in Palm Springs beyond the tourist strip?</h3>
<p>Yes. Thai Hot on Garnet Avenue, Rooster and the Pig in the Uptown Design District, and Rosemary Hi-Fi are all a few blocks off the main Palm Canyon corridor and noticeably less crowded on busy nights. Worth the five-minute walk from downtown.</p>

<h3>Do Palm Springs restaurants take reservations?</h3>
<p>Workshop, Trio, and Rosemary Hi-Fi take reservations and you should use them for weekends. Cheeky's and Thai Hot are walk-in only. Check each restaurant's Instagram for current policies since they change seasonally.</p>

<h3>Where should I eat in Palm Springs on a budget?</h3>
<p>Thai Hot is the best value on this list. Honest food, real portions, prices that won't make you think twice. The lunch menu at Workshop is also more accessible than dinner. For coffee and morning pastries, Koffi keeps it reasonable for a specialty coffee shop.</p>
